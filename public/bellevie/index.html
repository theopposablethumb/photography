Just enough support

Per Person not per hour

Unlike other care companies, here Bellevie we provide a simple easy to understand fixed price package rather than a price based on an hourly rate. This allows us to focus on your loved ones and not on the clock.

Get in touch

How do you communicate with my family?

We know that keeping in touch with family and friends is important, and together with your family we will look at a range of possible ways to help you stay connected. If you want to do this, we have many different options – for example, this could include a private and confidential Facebook group where we post photos and updates from you, and you family can also post and comment; it could be involve sharing learning and communication logs with your family, or testing or emailing photos. Privacy and confidentiality is very important to us, and so is helping you stay connected – we can do both. Family members can also have access to our care management app Birdie, to read notes about our visits.

What if I want to cancel my membership package?

You can cancel you monthly package at any time (and for any reason) by giving us two weeks) 14 days) notice.

What if I need to cancel visits?

If you do not need our services for an entire week or more, we will not charge for you that, as long as you let us know at least two weeks in advance. We do not reimburse you for cancellations of less than one week, except in the case of an unplanned hospital admission. In the case of cancellation for shorter periods than a week, we will do our best to offer you a longer visit at another time, or on another day, if the schedule of our Wellbeing Support Workers allows it.

How do you recruit and train Wellbeing Support Workers?

We recruit people with great value, and train them to have the right skills to support you in the way you want to be supported. Everyone follows a three month initial training and observation programme., exceeding the requirements of care certificate. This means that all our Wellbeing Support Workers can confident and competently deliver the support you need and work together as a supportive and effective team. Our Wellbeing Support Workers are Dementia Friends, and gain their level 2 qualification in medication.

However training is always ongoing, so should you require a suport around particular health or mobility needs we would source appropriate professional training, in order to support you safely. We follow a safe recruitment process, including DBS checks.

How many Wellbeing Support Workers will visit?

You will see different Wellbeing Support Workers from the same small, local team. You will have all of their names and pictures printed as part of your Welcome Pack, and you’ll be notified in advance of who is visiting you on a given day and time. If you have any issues or are not getting along with an individual Wellbeing Support Worker, please contact us to share your concern, and we will do our best to accommodate your preferences.

How long are the visits?

We know what has to be achieved on each visit, but our priority is achieving a positive outcome for you rather than watching the clock. Your Care and Support plan with give you a detailed list of the number and purpose of visits, and estimated minimum and maximum times for different types of visits. Short visits are to make sure someone is washed, dressed, and comfortable, then preparing and serving a meal are estimated to be around one hour. Longer visits, for example for social activities and housekeeping are estimated to be between one hour and three hours. We don’t do visits lasting under an hour unless specifically agreed in the care plan.

What if my needs and aspirations change?

We will review the number and length of visits you get every month, and we will discuss changing your package if you need more or less support from us.

How much does a monthly membership plan cost?

Following our free initial conversation with our Trusted Assessor at your home, we will offer you a customised monthly membership with the number of visits and outcomes you’d like to achieve. We use the number of visits and services provided to calculate the right membership price for you, at a competitive rate.

Where can I find your CQC Report?

BelleVie registered with the Care Quality Commission (CQC) in May 2020, and we are currently awaiting inspection. You can find our CQC page here. From June 2019 to May 2020, we worked in partnership with Wellbeing Teams, who were previously awarded a rating of outstanding bu the CQC (their report can be found here). We will continue to work closely with the CQC, whilst ensuring we provide outstanding care to those we support.

Our Care and Support Networks

We are members of several professional organisations, in order to work with other care providers, share best practices, and stay up to date on regulations. We form good working relationships with all other professionals involved in people’s care. We liaise on a daily basis with GP’s, Pharmacists, Occupational Therapists, District Nurses and with Age UK . We closely support people and their families when needs increase, and support them to move on when more intensive provision is required. Our teams are also experienced in working closely with the whole network, to provide end of life care, enabling people to remain at home with their families at this important time. We work hand-in-hand with the council when people we support are eligible for funding, and with the NHS Continuing Healthcare teams.

Certificate of safe, compassionate care and self management

Given that we recruit from outside the industry our training is comprehensive. We use both internal and external experts to cover all the mandatory elements required by CQC, plus training in the range of practices required for self management. We provide additional training in frequently encountered conditions such as Dementia and Parkinson's Disease. We also use associate professionals to provide bespoke training for teams around less common conditions or at end of life, so that we can support people in the most person centred way. During induction, as well as shadowing experienced staff, they complete a comprehensive suite of e-learning courses. They then undertake face to face training in first aid, safeguarding, medication and moving and assisting, before they can work independently. Observations of practice take place regularly during the workers probationary period.

  • We make life worth living.

  • We strive to be the best we can be by measuring outcomes.
  • We share accountability and take pride in everything we do.

Our Founders

Bellevie was born at London’s based social impact enterprise accelerator Zinc. Trudie and Violaine are both passionate about creating good jobs in care, to make it a desirable career that will attract top professionals and increase the quality of the service offered to our older population. They have interviewed hundreds of care workers and older people receiving care, and decided to create a new model for homecare that will answer their current concerns and struggles. BelleVie started in Abingdon in partnership with Wellbeing Teams and was joined in January 2020 by Mary Curran.

Our Inspiration

We have taken inspiration from Buurtzorg (self-managing teams of nurses in the Netherlands), Wellbeing Teams (self-managing teams in social care in England), and Cornerstone (self-managing teams care and support work in Scotland). We are particularly thankful to Wellbeing Teams for their generosity. They have shared so much with us, including self management techniques, documents and processes.
